Maya Angelou

A bird does not sing because it has an answer, it sings because it has a song.
Maya Angelou at the Clinton inauguration
  • Angelou recited her poem "On the Pulse of Morning" at the 1993 presidential inauguration of Bill Clinton. In doing so, Ms. Angelou became the first poet to make an inaugural recitation since Robert Frost at John F. Kennedy's 1961 inauguration.
  • At 16 years of age, she became the first Black female cable car conductor in San Francisco.
  • Ms. Angelou's danced and sang professionally to calypso music in clubs around San Francisco, including the nightclub The Purple Onion.
  • During 1954 and 1955, Ms. Angelou toured Europe with a production of the opera Porgy and Bess.
  • Ms. Angelou's screenplay, Georgia, Georgia (1972), was the first original script by a Black woman to be produced. She was the first Black woman to direct a major motion picture, Down in the Delta, in 1998.
